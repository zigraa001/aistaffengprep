Module 2: Interview Process Patterns

Learning Objectives

  • Recognize three main interview style groups
  • Understand evaluation criteria differences
  • Adapt preparation strategy by style
  • Manage different round types

Three Interview Style Groups

Note on Groupings

These groupings reflect dominant patterns as of early 2026. Most companies blend elements from multiple groups. Additionally, staff-level loops at every company tend to add architecture and leadership rounds on top of the base process. Always verify the current process with your recruiter.

Not shown above: Major companies like Google, Meta, Amazon, Apple, and Microsoft use hybrid approaches combining algorithmic coding, system design, and behavioral rounds. These are covered separately in Modules 3-5.

Group A: Non-LeetCode Practical ▼

Companies: Stripe, Netflix

Characteristics:

  • Real-world problem scenarios (e.g., Stripe's "build a payments feature" style)
  • Multi-part questions that evolve over 45-60 minutes
  • Code quality, readability, and extensibility over algorithmic complexity
  • Testing and edge cases evaluated
  • At staff level: expect follow-up questions on how you'd scale, deploy, and monitor your solution

Preparation Focus:

  • Build actual systems (parsers, caches, rate limiters)
  • Practice writing production-quality code under time pressure
  • Focus on code organization, error handling, and testing
  • For staff level: practice explaining trade-offs and alternative approaches as you code
Group B: Research & Work-Sample ▼

Companies: Anthropic, OpenAI

Characteristics:

  • Take-home or work-sample projects that test real engineering judgment
  • Anthropic uses multi-stage interviews including a research-flavored technical screen, a work trial (paid, 1-2 days of realistic work), and cross-functional interviews
  • OpenAI has shifted toward practical coding and system design rounds with emphasis on ML infrastructure and product engineering; processes change frequently -- confirm with your recruiter
  • Deep domain knowledge expected (ML systems, safety, infrastructure)
  • At staff level: expect questions on organizational decision-making and technical strategy

Preparation Focus:

  • Practice building well-structured solutions under realistic conditions (not just timed puzzles)
  • For Anthropic: prepare for the paid work trial by practicing working on unfamiliar codebases quickly
  • For OpenAI: study ML infrastructure patterns, model serving, and distributed training concepts
  • Build modular, reusable code with clear documentation
Group C: Systems Heavy ▼

Companies: Databricks, Snowflake

Characteristics:

  • Deep technical knowledge expected (storage engines, query optimization, distributed consensus)
  • Database/distributed systems focus -- expect questions on internals, not just usage
  • Presentation or "deep dive" rounds where you present a past project's architecture (especially at staff level)
  • Trade-off discussions: why did you choose X over Y?
  • At staff level: Databricks may include a "tech talk" round where you present a topic of your choice

Preparation Focus:

  • Review distributed systems concepts: consensus protocols, partitioning, replication
  • Understand database internals: LSM trees, B-trees, query planning, columnar vs. row storage
  • Practice technical presentations: prepare a 30-min deep dive on a system you built
  • Study Apache Spark internals (for Databricks) or cloud-native data warehouse architecture (for Snowflake)
