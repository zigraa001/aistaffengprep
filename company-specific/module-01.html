Module 1: Company Research Framework

Learning Objectives

  • Apply the CORE research method
  • Use key resources effectively
  • Build a comprehensive research checklist
  • Prioritize research time
  • Understand how staff-level research differs from senior-level

Why Staff-Level Research Is Different

At the staff level, interviewers evaluate organizational impact, technical vision, and cross-team leadership -- not just coding ability. Your research must go deeper:

  • Org structure matters: Understand the reporting chain, how staff engineers are positioned relative to engineering managers, and whether the company uses a "staff-plus" ladder or architect track.
  • Scope of influence: Research whether staff engineers at that company own org-wide technical direction, lead RFCs, or operate as embedded experts within a single team.
  • Recent technical bets: Know the company's major architectural decisions in the last 12-18 months (e.g., migration to a new platform, adoption of AI tooling, infrastructure rewrites).

C - Culture Research ▼

What to Research:

  • Company values and mission statement
  • Engineering principles (if published)
  • Culture deck or manifesto (Netflix, etc.)
  • Employee reviews on Glassdoor

Key Questions:

  • What behaviors are rewarded?
  • How do teams make decisions?
  • What's the communication style?
O - Organization Research ▼

What to Research:

  • Org structure and reporting lines
  • Team you'd be joining
  • Company size and growth stage
  • Tech stack and engineering blog

Where to Look:

  • LinkedIn - team composition, staff engineer profiles, reporting lines
  • Engineering blog - technical philosophy, architecture decisions
  • GitHub - open-source projects, coding standards, RFC processes
  • Levels.fyi - leveling data to understand staff vs. senior expectations
  • Conference talks - staff engineers from the company often present at QCon, StrangeLoop, or internal tech talks posted publicly
R - Recent News ▼

What to Research:

  • Recent product launches
  • Company announcements
  • Technical blog posts (last 6 months)
  • Industry news and challenges

Why It Matters:

Shows you're genuinely interested and up-to-date. Great for asking informed questions.

E - Evaluation (Interview Process) ▼

What to Research:

  • Interview round structure
  • Question types and style
  • Unique rounds (integration, presentation)
  • Evaluation criteria

Where to Look:

  • Glassdoor interview section (filter for "Staff Engineer" or "L6/L7" titles)
  • Blind (Fishbowl) discussions -- search for company-specific channels
  • Levels.fyi interview tips and offer data
  • HelloInterview and interviewing.io -- mock interview recordings at senior/staff level

Staff-Level Additions to Watch For:

  • Architecture/design presentation rounds (common at Databricks, Snowflake, Netflix)
  • "Bar raiser" or cross-functional interview rounds
  • Leadership and influence behavioral rounds with higher expectations than senior level
  • Team-matching or hiring manager deep-dives on past impact

Research Checklist Template

Read company values/culture page
Review 5+ engineering blog posts
Check Glassdoor interviews (last 6 months)
Understand interview round structure
Identify unique round types
Prepare 3-5 questions to ask them
