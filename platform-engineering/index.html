Platform Engineering Interview Prep

Master the five pillars of platform engineering interviews: fundamentals applied to real systems, connectivity & API integration, comprehensive QA & testing, effective code review, and scenario-based system thinking.

Course Progress 0% Complete

Course Philosophy

  • Five-pillar mastery — Fundamentals, Connectivity, Testing, Code Review, and Scenario Thinking
  • Platform-as-product thinking — Internal developer platforms are products; treat them that way
  • Q&A active recall format — All content as questions with collapsible answers for interview readiness
  • Real code, real systems — Working examples in Python, Go, Java, TypeScript with production patterns
  • Staff-level scope — Strategy, cross-team impact, and architectural decision-making

Who This Course Is For

  • Staff and principal engineers interviewing for platform engineering roles
  • Senior engineers who want to demonstrate API design, testing strategy, and code review expertise
  • Engineers moving from product teams to internal developer platform teams
  • Anyone preparing for interviews that assess connectivity management, QA depth, and production thinking

Key Themes Throughout

Platform as Product

Internal developer platforms serve developer customers. Treat abstractions, APIs, and golden paths as products with SLOs and feedback loops.

Connectivity Mastery

Webhooks, events, message brokers, resilience patterns — the connective tissue of modern distributed systems.

Testing as Architecture

Testing strategy is an architectural decision. The right shape, the right level, the right tradeoffs for your system.

Review as Teaching

Code review at staff level is mentorship, standard-setting, and security gatekeeping rolled into one practice.
